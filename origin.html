Ethiopian Highlands

1,900–2,200 meters above sea level — where exceptional coffee is born.

The Perfect Growing Conditions

Our coffee is cultivated by small-scale farmers in traditional garden farms across Ethiopia's highlands. The cool highland climate, natural shade trees, fertile volcanic soil, and balanced seasonal rainfall allow the coffee cherries to ripen slowly, developing refined and complex flavors.

These conditions are unique to Ethiopia and cannot be replicated elsewhere. The slow maturation process at high altitudes concentrates sugars and acids, creating the distinctive brightness and floral character Ethiopian coffee is famous for worldwide.

Cup Profile

Elegant · Bright · Complex · Smooth

  • ✓ Floral aroma — notes of jasmine, bergamot, and wildflowers
  • ✓ Bright acidity — lively, refreshing, wine-like qualities
  • ✓ Citrus and fruit notes — lemon, bergamot, red berry, stone fruit
  • ✓ Smooth clean finish — lingering sweetness with no bitterness
Smallholder Farmers

Traditional Garden Farming

Our coffee comes from family-owned farms where coffee grows naturally under shade trees alongside other crops.

Altitude

1,900–2,200m

High altitude slows cherry maturation, developing more complex sugars and organic acids for exceptional flavor.

Processing

Washed & Natural

Depending on the lot, we offer both washed (for clarity) and natural (for fruit-forward) processed coffees from partner farms.

Harvest

October – January

Cherries are hand-picked at peak ripeness, sorted, and processed within hours to preserve freshness.

Traceability & Transparency

We believe you should know exactly where your coffee comes from. Each bag of Bean to Bag coffee includes the region, altitude, processing method, roast date, and batch number.

Our long-term goal is to invest in dry-processing coffee land, allowing us to control quality from cherry to export and build direct relationships with farming communities.
