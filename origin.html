The Cradle of Arabica

Our coffee is cultivated by small-scale farmers in traditional garden farms at 1,900–2,200 meters above sea level. The cool highland climate, natural shade trees, fertile volcanic soil, and balanced seasonal rainfall allow the coffee cherries to ripen slowly, developing refined and complex flavors that cannot be replicated elsewhere.

✦ 100% Coffea Arabica ✦

Heirloom varieties indigenous to Ethiopia — Typica, Bourbon, and local landraces. Every bean is hand-picked, naturally processed or washed with pristine spring water. Our growers preserve centuries-old cultivation wisdom, yielding a cup that sings with elegance and depth.

Guardians of Tradition

More than 12 partner cooperatives, each family-owned farm averaging 1–2 hectares. The farmers practice intercropping with enset, maize, and fruit trees, creating a biodiverse ecosystem. Harvest is done meticulously, selecting only deep-red cherries, which are delivered to drying beds within hours.
