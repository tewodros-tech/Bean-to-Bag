OUR ETHOS

Coffee is memory. We protect it from highland to your cup.

Bean to Bag was born from a single belief: that specialty coffee should never lose its soul. We work directly with smallholder farmers in Ethiopia's Gedeo and Sidama zones — families who have cultivated heirloom Arabica for generations.

Every cherry is hand-picked, every lot is traceable, and every roast is a quiet conversation between the land and the roaster. We don't chase trends. We chase clarity, sweetness, and the kind of aroma that stops you mid-sentence.

LIVING TRADITION

The Ethiopian coffee ceremony: a ritual of connection

In Ethiopia, coffee is more than a drink — it is an invitation. The traditional ceremony involves washing green beans, roasting them over an open flame, grinding them by hand, and brewing in a clay pot called a jebena.

Three rounds of coffee are served: Abol, Tona, and Baraka. Each cup represents a blessing. At Bean to Bag, we carry that spirit into every bag we seal.

MASTERY IN EVERY BATCH

Restrained roasting. Maximum expression.

Our approach is minimal and precise: develop sweetness, preserve florals, and never hide the origin behind roast flavours. We use small-batch roasters that allow us to adjust curves for each lot.

Every bag includes a one-way valve, roast date, grind selection, and full traceability. Because great coffee should arrive as intended: alive, aromatic, and unforgettable.
