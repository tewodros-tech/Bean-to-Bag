Our Ethos

Coffee is memory. We protect it from highland to your cup.

Bean to Bag was born from a single belief: that specialty coffee should never lose its soul. We work directly with smallholder farmers in Ethiopia's Gedeo and Sidama zones — families who have cultivated heirloom Arabica for generations. Every cherry is hand-picked, every lot is traceable, and every roast is a quiet conversation between the land and the roaster.

We don't chase trends. We chase clarity, sweetness, and the kind of aroma that stops you mid-sentence. Our name says it all: from the moment the bean is harvested to the moment you tear open the bag, we're guardians of that journey.

Living Tradition

The Ethiopian coffee ceremony: a ritual of connection

In Ethiopia, coffee is more than a drink — it is an invitation. The traditional ceremony, led by a woman of the household, involves washing green beans, roasting them over an open flame, grinding them by hand, and brewing in a clay pot called a jebena. The aroma of frankincense fills the room as three rounds of coffee are served: Abol, Tona, and Baraka. Each cup represents a blessing.

At Bean to Bag, we carry that spirit into every bag we seal. It's why we roast with care, package with intention, and invite you to slow down and truly taste the story.

Mastery in Every Batch

Restrained roasting. Maximum expression.

Our approach is minimal and precise: develop sweetness, preserve florals, and never hide the origin behind roast flavours. We use small-batch roasters that allow us to adjust curves for each lot — whether it's a bright, tea-like washed coffee or a honey-processed gem with wild berry notes.

Every bag includes a one-way valve, roast date, grind selection, and full traceability. Because great coffee should arrive as intended: alive, aromatic, and unforgettable.
