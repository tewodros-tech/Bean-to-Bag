Origin & Terroir

Ethiopian Highlands 1,900–2,200m

Cultivated by small-scale farmers in traditional garden farms under natural shade. Slow ripening yields refined floral aromas, bright acidity, and citrus & fruit notes. Our coffee represents the true essence of Ethiopian Arabica.

Cup Profile
Floral aroma · Citrus · Bright acidity · Clean finish
Cool Climate
Natural shade · Balanced rainfall · Volcanic soil

Roast Profile: Medium-Dark

Balanced acidity, full body, rich chocolate notes — versatile for Jebena, espresso, French press, and milk-based drinks. Designed to honor Ethiopian tradition while delivering a modern specialty experience.

Bean to Bag Vision

From Small Batch to Global Brand

Year 1 · Establish

200kg/month, build café & office partnerships, direct sales via social media.

Year 2 · Expand

Scale to 500kg/month, invest in dry-processing land to reduce costs & control quality.

Year 3 · Global

Open retail shop in Addis, begin exporting roasted Ethiopian coffee internationally.

Long-term Supply Chain Control

Within 1–2 years, Bean to Bag will invest in dry-processing land to directly manage natural processing, select cherries, lower green bean cost and improve traceability — from farm to bag.
