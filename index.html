Birthplace of coffee

Ancient highlands,
timeless flavour

Ethiopia's Gedeo zone and Yirgacheffe region are the cradle of Arabica. Our partner farmers use traditional agroforestry, interplanting coffee with enset and fruit trees. The mineral-rich volcanic soil, combined with misty mornings and sunny afternoons, yields beans of extraordinary complexity. Each micro-lot is traceable to washing stations where cherries are meticulously floated and dried on raised African beds for 12–18 days, unlocking nuanced notes of jasmine, peach, and bergamot.

Shade-Grown & Forest Friendly

Canopy trees protect coffee from heat stress, extend cherry maturation, and boost sweetness. Over 90% of our beans are organic by traditional methods.
