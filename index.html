A Culture of Coffee Ceremony

In Ethiopia, coffee is more than a drink — it's a sacred ritual of connection. The traditional Jebena ceremony involves roasting beans over an open flame, grinding by hand, and brewing three rounds of coffee, each with its own blessing.

We bring this spirit of hospitality into every bag, connecting you directly to the farmers and traditions behind each bean.

The Ethiopian Coffee Journey

The Jebena Ritual

The coffee ceremony is the heart of Ethiopian hospitality. Fresh beans are roasted in a pan over charcoal, filling the room with an intoxicating aroma. The roasted beans are then ground using a mortar and pestle, then brewed in a clay pot called a Jebena. This timeless ritual symbolizes friendship, respect, and the soul of the nation.

Master Roasting

Small-batch roasting is an art form. We use a precision Loring roaster to carefully develop each bean's potential — unlocking notes of wild berries, jasmine, dark chocolate, and a winey acidity. Our medium-dark profile honors Ethiopian tradition while delivering a smooth, balanced cup that works beautifully for espresso, filter, or Jebena.
